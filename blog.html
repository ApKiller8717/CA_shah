RERA at a Glance

Why RERA Introduce?

The RERA (Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016) was introduced to bring transparency, accountability, and efficiency to the Indian real estate sector, ultimately protecting the interests of homebuyers. Prior to RERA, the sector was largely unregulated, leading to issues like delayed projects, misrepresentation of project details, and misuse of funds by developers.

Here's a more detailed breakdown of the reasons behind RERA's introduction:

  • Protecting Homebuyers
  • Promoting Transparency and Accountability
  • Ensuring Financial Discipline
  • Establishing Regulatory Authorities
  • Standardizing Practices
  • Boosting Investor Confidence

RERA Applicability

As Per Section 3(2) of RERA provide the exemption to real estate project from registration requirement and the conditions are as follows:

Size:

Projects with a land area exceeding 500 square meters or those with more than 8 apartments are required to register under RERA.

Type:

Both residential and commercial real estate projects fall under RERA's purview.

Redevelopment:

Redevelopment projects may also require RERA registration, particularly if they involve selling new apartments, developing more than 500 square meters, or constructing more than 8 units.

Planning Area:

The term "planning area" broadly encompasses areas covered by Town Planning Schemes, Special Investment Regions, and GIDC estates. Projects located within these planning areas are generally required to register with Gujarat RERA.

Exemptions:

Projects that are only undergoing renovation, repair, or redevelopment, without any new advertising, marketing, selling, or allotment of units, may be exempt from RERA registration, and projects that have already received completion certificates before RERA introduce.

Restriction before RERA registration

Section 3 of RERA states the requirement of registration of real estate project with RERA Authority and restriction on sale, booking, advertisement marketing etc. without registration of real estate project.

This Section restrict the promoter from the following activities, in relation to plot, apartment building in a real estate project which is developed in planning area, without registering the real estate project:

  • Advertisement
  • Marketing
  • Booking
  • Sale
  • Offer to Sale
  • Invitation to purchase

RERA Registration Process at glance

  1. User Registration: Promoters must first create a user account on the GUJRERA website by providing company details and authorized representative information.
  2. Project Details: Select "Project Registration" and choose the appropriate promoter type (individual, company, etc.). Provide detailed project information, including location, area, and number of units.
  3. Document Submission: Upload necessary documents as per the RERA checklist, which can be found on the GUJRERA website.
  4. Fee Payment: Pay the required registration fees based on the project size and location.
  5. Scrutiny and Approval: The GUJRERA authorities will review the application and documents. Additional information or clarifications might be requested.
  6. Registration Confirmation: Upon successful verification, the project will be assigned a unique registration number.

Filing of Returns - Quarterly Processing Return(QPR)

Quarterly Submission:
Developers must submit QPRs every three months for each registered project.
Content of QPR:
The return includes information on the original approved plans, any modifications, revenue collected, fund utilization, construction progress, and expected delivery dates.
Certifications:
QPRs require certifications from an architect and engineer, verifying the project's progress and financial details.
Timeline and Deadlines:
Developers must adhere to the deadlines for submitting QPRs, and failure to do so can result in penalties.
Penalty for Delay:
GujRERA is implementing daily fines for delayed QPR submissions, with the penalty amount varying based on the project size.

Alteration/Extension in RERA - Is it possible ?

Yes, alterations and extensions are possible under GUJRERA (Gujarat Real Estate Regulatory Authority), but they require specific procedures and approvals. Promoters must apply for these changes, and depending on the nature and timing, there may be fees and potential regulatory action.

Alterations:

  • Minor Changes: Architects or engineers can make minor changes (as recommended) after informing the allottee.
  • Major Changes: For significant alterations, the promoter needs consent from at least two-thirds of the allottees and prior permission from the RERA Authority.
  • Application Process: Promoters need to apply to GUJRERA for alterations, especially if there are revised plans.
  • Timing: Applications for alteration should be made within 3 months of the revised plans being approved, otherwise late fees may apply.

Extensions:

  • Application: Promoters can apply for project extensions, especially if the end date is approaching.
  • Timing: Applications should be made before the project's end date, and late fees may apply for applications made closer to or after the end date.
  • Reasons for Extension: Extensions can be sought due to reasonable circumstances or force majeure events, with the authority deciding on the extension and applicable fees.
